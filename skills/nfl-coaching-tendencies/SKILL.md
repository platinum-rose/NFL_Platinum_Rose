# NFL Coaching Tendencies — Reference Guide

> **Load this skill when:** analyzing a specific coach's tendencies, 4th-down decision edges,
> 2-minute drill quality, or HC-driven situational angles in support of a bet.

## How to Use This Skill

1. Identify relevant head coach(es) for the matchup
2. Check their profile below for applicable tendencies
3. Use tendencies as supporting evidence — not standalone triggers
4. Cross-reference with current-season data; scheme/staff changes invalidate old profiles
5. When in doubt: check `references/` for deeper historical ATS data

## Coaching Profiles

### Andy Reid — Kansas City Chiefs

- **Identity:** Elite offensive architect; scheme variety, pace control, pre-snap deception
- **4th down:** Aggressive — top-5 league attempt rate; trusts offense in short-yardage
- **2-minute drill:** Elite — Mahomes + Reid is the best unit in the league at late-game drives
- **Game script:** Up-tempo when trailing; adapts personnel to opponent's weaknesses each week
- **Betting angle:** Chiefs rarely get blown out; backdoor cover risk is real — they score late even in
  garbage time. Factor into first-half spreads vs. full-game spreads.
- **ATS watch:** Chiefs cover as road favorites more reliably than home favorites when spread is 7+.
  Home favorites of 10+ have historically been a fade opportunity.

---

### Kyle Shanahan — San Francisco 49ers

- **Identity:** Motion-heavy RPO offense; run-establishing identity; pre-snap displacement
- **4th down:** Moderate — relies more on field position than 4th-down aggression
- **2-minute drill:** Below average without an elite improvising QB; scheme-dependent, not spontaneous
- **Game script:** Must run to set up play-action; becomes one-dimensional if lead RB is limited
- **Betting angle:** 49ers totals skew LOW when lead RB is questionable or out.
  Passing game loses the play-action setup that makes it efficient.
- **ATS watch:** Shanahan teams historically under-cover as large favorites (10+) — they control
  the clock but don't pile on. Prefer to fade the blowout narrative.

---

### Mike Tomlin — Pittsburgh Steelers

- **Identity:** Culture-first coach; defense-anchored; extreme consistency (never had a losing season)
- **4th down:** Conservative — punts more than average; trusts defense to hold
- **2-minute drill:** Varies with QB quality; historically strong with elite QBs (Roethlisberger era)
- **Game script:** Builds early leads; relies on defense to close; uncomfortable in shootouts
- **Betting angle:** Tomlin teams almost never tank — historically strong ATS record as underdogs,
  particularly in divisional games. Steelers +7 or more is often a sharp-money side.
- **ATS watch:** Watch for regression in non-divisional road games when Steelers are short underdogs
  and facing high-powered offenses.

---

### Sean McVay — Los Angeles Rams

- **Identity:** Pre-snap motion architect; identifies coverages via pre-snap motion before snap
- **4th down:** Aggressive — uses motion to simplify 4th-and-short reads
- **2-minute drill:** Good — fast tempo, pre-snap communication built into system
- **Game script:** Pass-heavy; can fall behind if run defense collapses
- **Betting angle:** McVay teams tend to cover more as home favorites. Pre-snap motion creates
  coverage confusion that produces chunk plays. Totals skew high in dome/neutral weather.
- **ATS watch:** McVay has a below-average record ATS as a road underdog. Road adversity + scheme
  disruption tends to hurt the motion-heavy system.

---

### Matt LaFleur — Green Bay Packers

- **Identity:** Shanahan disciple; motion-heavy; QB-dependent scheme
- **4th down:** Increasingly aggressive with Jordan Love as starter
- **2-minute drill:** Good — Packers have been efficient in late-game drive situations under Love
- **Betting angle:** Packers totals have historically been volatile — good passing weather + dome =
  lean over; cold Lambeau + division rival = lean under
- **ATS watch:** LaFleur is uneven early in season (inconsistent offensive identity in weeks 1-4);
  strong ATS finisher in weeks 14-17 stretch runs.

---

### Nick Sirianni — Philadelphia Eagles

- **Identity:** Run-first when it works; adjusts scheme to roster; aggressive special teams
- **4th down:** Aggressive (especially with Hurts at QB — scramble threat reduces the risk)
- **Betting angle:** Eagles run game + Hurts scramble = ATS edge as home favorites in moderate weather.
  Road games in cold December in NFC North are fade candidates.

---

### Sean McDermott — Buffalo Bills

- **Identity:** Defense-first culture builder; disciplined with strong situational awareness
- **4th down:** Moderate-aggressive — trusts Josh Allen's improvisation on short-yardage; lets Allen freelance more than most DCs-turned-HCs
- **2-minute drill:** Elite — Allen is the best in the league in unscripted 2-min situations; McDermott enables rather than restricts
- **Game script:** Builds early with run + play-action; goes no-huddle hurry-up when trailing
- **Betting angle:** Bills in cold home weather (November–January) are excellent ATS performers; home underdog situations historically very strong (McDermott culture = never quit)
- **ATS watch:** Bills as road favorites of 7+ have underperformed historically. McDermott-led playoff games ATS have been poor — team tends to get tight in elimination spots.

---

### Mike McDaniel — Miami Dolphins

- **Identity:** Shanahan disciple; wide-zone run blocking scheme; speed-first personnel; relies on pre-snap motion to manufacture yards
- **4th down:** Aggressive — uses RPO to simplify reads in short yardage
- **2-minute drill:** Good — Tua Tagovailoa is accurate and decisive in short/intermediate windows
- **Game script:** Offense runs best when establishing the run first; Tua becomes more efficient when play-action is available
- **Betting angle:** **Critical situational angle:** Dolphins in September/October home games in Miami heat = strong ATS edge (heat disadvantage for opposing teams is real). Cold weather road games (below 40°F) are extreme fade candidates — Miami is historically one of the worst cold-weather teams in the league.
- **ATS watch:** Totals in cold-weather venues lean heavily UNDER for Miami. Weather report must be checked before any Dolphins pick.

---

### Mike Vrabel — New England Patriots

- **Identity:** Physical, defense-first; discipline and accountability culture; built Titans into an annual underdog machine; arrived in NE 2025
- **4th down:** Conservative — trusts defense to hold; minimizes risk in field-position game
- **2-minute drill:** QB-dependent; historically managed with efficient, not explosive, quarterbacks
- **Game script:** Tries to control time of possession; physical ball control; avoids explosive plays allowed
- **Betting angle:** Vrabel teams almost never get blown out — extreme low variance on defense. Value as home underdog when public fades the Patriots. His Titans teams had historically strong ATS records as underdogs.
- **ATS watch:** First-year NE rebuild — expect inconsistency as roster is reconfigured; manage expectations on favorites.

### Aaron Glenn — New York Jets

- **Identity:** Defensive coordinator background (Lions under Campbell); Cover-2/Cover-4 pressure packager; physical and disciplined
- **4th down:** Conservative — trusts defense to hold field position
- **2-minute drill:** TBD with developing QB situation in 2025
- **Game script:** Wants to establish defensive identity first; offense will be run-first early in tenure
- **Betting angle:** Jets defense under Glenn should be a strong unit — fade totals in close games early in season as offense establishes identity; Jets as home underdogs when public overreacts
- **ATS watch:** Very limited HC data (Year 1 in 2025); profile requires updating through 2025 season.

### John Harbaugh — Baltimore Ravens

- **Identity:** Elite situational coach; physical culture; willing to make unconventional bold calls (onside kicks, 4th-and-long in key moments); runs RPO-heavy Lamar Jackson system
- **4th down:** Aggressive — one of the early adopters of 4th-down aggressiveness in the NFL; Lamar's rushing threat makes 4th-and-short a near-certainty
- **2-minute drill:** Elite — Lamar Jackson makes this the most dangerous 2-min offense in the AFC; Harbaugh enables the RPO scramble clock exploitation
- **Game script:** Establishes run game first; uses Lamar as a ball-control and explosive threat; rarely abandons the run
- **Betting angle:** Ravens as road underdogs of 3-7 points are historically a sharp-money target — team culture and Lamar guarantee competitiveness. Ravens large home favorites (14+) tend to under-cover due to game management conservatism late.
- **ATS watch:** Harbaugh manages scores with a big lead — will run the clock instead of padding. Home blowout ATS has historically been a weakness.

### Kevin Stefanski — Cleveland Browns

- **Identity:** West Coast run-game base; RPO integration; run-first, PA pass second; adaptable to QB skill sets
- **4th down:** Moderate — tends toward field position in hostile environments
- **2-minute drill:** Inconsistent — heavily QB-dependent; has been subpar without an elite playmaker
- **Game script:** Most effective with a run-capable QB and dual-threat threat; becomes one-dimensional in a passing environment when RBs are limited
- **Betting angle:** Browns in bad weather (snow, cold) at home are historically strong ATS performers — the run game advantage is maximized. When weather is neutral, Browns totals lean UNDER due to offensive limitations.
- **ATS watch:** Browns as favorites of 3+ with a strong home opponent or divisional road game are a systemic fade. Stefanski's offense hasn't demonstrated elite ceiling in prime-time games.

### Zac Taylor — Cincinnati Bengals

- **Identity:** McVay disciple; pass-first; route concept variety; Burrow-dependent; aggressive in red zone
- **4th down:** Aggressive — trusts Burrow's quick-release and pick-routes
- **2-minute drill:** Elite when Burrow is healthy — one of the best units in the league in structured 2-min passing
- **Game script:** Pass-first from the start; establishes tempo early; relies on Burrow to win from behind
- **Betting angle:** Bengals with healthy Burrow = totals lean OVER; elite passing offense combined with defense that gives up yards creates high-scoring games. Bengals as underdogs historically very strong ATS — Taylor's teams have been systematically undervalued by the market.
- **ATS watch:** Burrow injury risk is the #1 Bengals ATS variable. Without him, the offense drops multiple tiers and totals plummet.

### DeMeco Ryans — Houston Texans

- **Identity:** Defense-first; multiple-front 4-2-5 structure; disguises coverages well; former All-Pro LB; builds from defensive identity
- **4th down:** Conservative-moderate — still establishing culture; defers to punter in non-obvious situations
- **2-minute drill:** Stroud is developing; adequate but not elite in manufactured drives
- **Game script:** Wants to play ahead; struggles when trailing and needing to score fast
- **Betting angle:** Texans defense creates turnovers — pay attention to points off turnovers in their covers. When defense is active, Texans as moderate underdogs have ATS value.
- **ATS watch:** Texans as large favorites (10+) have been unreliable — offense doesn't pile on, and defense can't control margin in high-offense matchups.

### Brian Callahan — Tennessee Titans

- **Identity:** Shanahan-influenced motion offense; wide zone base; QB developer (mentored Herbert in Cincinnati under Taylor); building Titans' post-Henry identity
- **4th down:** Moderate — adapting to roster rebuild
- **2-minute drill:** Team is in transition; limited explosive playmakers constrains the ceiling
- **Game script:** Run-establish identity; will be ground-and-pound when personnel allows
- **Betting angle:** Titans are in a rebuild phase; fade as favorites in competitive matchups; home underdog value when defense is healthy and opponent is overvalued
- **ATS watch:** Year 2+ upswing expected as Callahan installs system — monitor preseason to identify when offense becomes competent.

### Shane Steichen — Indianapolis Colts

- **Identity:** Elite QB developer (Josh Allen in BUF, Herbert in LAC, Hurts in PHI); creative IDP/RPO scheme; offensive identity built around QB mobility
- **4th down:** Aggressive — trusts his QB + RPO architecture to convert short yardage
- **2-minute drill:** When Richardson is healthy, good — scramble ability extends drives; without Richardson, drops significantly
- **Game script:** Must establish run to open up the play-action that defines the offense
- **Betting angle:** **Anthony Richardson health is the single biggest ATS variable in the AFC South.** When Richardson plays a full game, Colts totals lean over. When he's absent or limited, extreme under lean. Check injury report Wednesday–Friday before locking any Colts bet.
- **ATS watch:** Steichen's Colts have been a solid ATS team as home underdogs; the public underrates them when coming off a blowout loss.

### Liam Coen — Jacksonville Jaguars

- **Identity:** Air Raid background (Rams, Kentucky); aggressive downfield passing; pass-first identity; McVay coaching tree
- **4th down:** Aggressive — scheme is built to exploit favorable math
- **2-minute drill:** Good — Air Raid principles translate well to hurry-up
- **Game script:** Pass-first from the start; not run-heavy; looks to establish quick tempo
- **Betting angle:** Totals lean OVER with Coen's system — Air Raid produces volume. Jaguars pass defense vulnerabilities create high-scoring games. Value early in season as market adjusts to new HC.

### Pete Carroll — Las Vegas Raiders

- **Identity:** Culture-first; defense-first philosophy; Cover-3 base zone; demands physical play; returned from retirement to coach Raiders in 2025
- **4th down:** Conservative — trusts defense and field position over explosive offense
- **2-minute drill:** Conservative — runs clock when ahead; will punt aggressively in own territory
- **Game script:** Wants to play ahead early; defense-anchored identity means low-scoring game preference
- **Betting angle:** Carroll teams as underdogs have historically been a strong ATS play — his culture demands competitiveness even in rebuilds. Early 2025 season in a rebuild = extra underdog value.
- **ATS watch:** Totals in Carroll-coached games lean UNDER when defense is functional. Raiders home games in domed stadium = some weather neutrality but Carroll's conservative style still drags totals down.

### Sean Payton — Denver Broncos

- **Identity:** Elite offensive mind; precision timing passing game; aggressive red zone; one of the best clock managers in NFL history; Saints architect for 15 years
- **4th down:** Aggressive
- **2-minute drill:** Elite — Payton's 2-min management is among the best in NFL history; designs plays specifically for end-of-half scenarios
- **Game script:** Creates scripted openers; dictates tempo; adapts beautifully to in-game situations
- **Betting angle:** Payton's Denver home games at altitude = weather-neutral advantage but thin-air factor. Payton historically covers as a road underdog when he has a competent QB. First-half totals lean slightly over with Payton's scripted offensive openers.
- **ATS watch:** The Payton-Russell Wilson mismatch (2023-2024) suppressed his ATS record; with Bo Nix developing, expect improvement. Watch for strong Weeks 8-14 run when system is fully installed.

### Jim Harbaugh — Los Angeles Chargers

- **Identity:** Punishing run-first identity; old-school complement football; physical culture; Michigan-style power running brought to NFL
- **4th down:** Aggressive — Harbaugh's Michigan teams were historically top-5 in attempt rate; brings same philosophy to LAC
- **2-minute drill:** Good — prefers clock management but is capable of structured 2-min drives; Herbert enables this efficiently
- **Game script:** Run-establish first; use Herbert in controlled downfield throws; not a throw-it-every-play coach
- **Betting angle:** **Totals lean UNDER** in Harbaugh-coached games vs. offensive coordinators — he constrains the game script. Chargers as home underdogs 3-6 with Herbert = historically strong ATS signal.
- **ATS watch:** Harbaugh's teams historically cover as underdogs more often than as favorites; public tends to over-rate the Chargers based on talent alone without accounting for Harbaugh's conservative game management.

- **Game script:** Run to set up play-action; Dak Prescott's strengths align with the scheme when healthy

### Dan Quinn — Washington Commanders

- **4th down:** Moderate — defense-first philosophy means he trusts his D more than 4th-down aggression
- **2-minute drill:** Jayden Daniels is capable and mobile; Quinn gives him space to extend plays

- **Betting angle:** Commanders at home in 2025 with a developing Daniels = undervalued by market; Dan Quinn culture rebuild teams historically show strong ATS improvement in Year 1-2. NFC East division games are Quinn's specialty.

### Ben Johnson — Chicago Bears

- **Identity:** Elite offensive mind (Lions' architect under Campbell); motion-heavy, chunk-play focused; one of the most creative OCs in the league before becoming HC; built scheme around Caleb Williams for 2025
- **4th down:** Aggressive — Lions offense under his OC tenure was top-2 in 4th-down attempt rate; expects to replicate in CHI
- **2-minute drill:** Elite — Williams' arm + improvisation + Johnson's scripted end-of-half series = dangerous 2-min unit
- **Game script:** Pass-volume heavy; creates chunks via pre-snap motion and pick concepts; will establish run when defense allows
- **Betting angle:** Totals lean OVER in Bears home games with Williams healthy — Johnson's scheme creates scoring. Bears as underdogs in divisional games where public fades them = value. Cold Soldier Field games create no disadvantage for Johnson's system.
- **ATS watch:** Year 1 HC growing pains are possible but offensive identity is established; watch for improvement in Weeks 8-17.

### Dan Campbell — Detroit Lions

- **Identity:** The most aggressive head coach in the NFL by 4th-down attempt rate; physical culture; offense built around Jared Goff's precision + explosive skill players; "bite kneecaps" culture
- **4th down:** **Top-1 or top-2 in the league every season.** Will attempt 4th-and-5 from opponent's 40 in the first quarter. Not a gimmick — it's the scheme. Never assume Campbell will punt in reasonable field position.
- **2-minute drill:** Excellent — Lions offense is one of the most explosive in the league; Goff is elite in structured 2-min drives; St. Brown/ARSB provide reliable targets
- **Game script:** Never abandons aggression regardless of score; will score even from ahead by 17
- **Betting angle:** **Lions totals lean OVER** — Campbell will not stop scoring even with a big lead. Strong ATS as home favorites because of the aggression. If Lions lead at halftime, they do not turtle — they keep attacking.
- **ATS watch:** Lions as large favorites (14+) may actually still cover because Campbell won't take his foot off the gas. Total and spread both skew aggressive with Campbell.

- **4th down:** Aggressive
- **2-minute drill:** Excellent — designed around WR speed + accurate QB; Justin Jefferson guarantees volume in 2-min
- **Game script:** Pass-volume first; run game used to keep defenses honest; Jefferson is the scheme's apex weapon
- **Betting angle:** Vikings totals lean moderate-high; O'Connell creates touches for multiple skill players. Vikings as home underdogs in divisional games = historically excellent ATS signal under O'Connell.
- **ATS watch:** Vikings with McCarthy developing = Year 1 volatility; expect higher efficiency in Weeks 8+.

### Todd Bowles — Tampa Bay Buccaneers

### Jonathan Gannon — Arizona Cardinals

### Mike Macdonald — Seattle Seahawks

## Update Protocol

Update this file when:

- A head coaching change occurs (any week — immediate invalidation of that team's profile)
- A coordinator leaves (OC departure = recalibrate 4th-down and 2-min drill data)
- A 3+ game streak contradicts a stated tendency (update "ATS watch" note)
- After each bye week (post-bye scheme adjustments are common)
